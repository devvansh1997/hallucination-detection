\section{Dataset Overview}

All datasets generated with the full 10-beam HARP-compatible protocol.
Hidden states extracted from all generated beams, mean-pooled across tokens,
saved as $(L, D)$ tensors.  Labels assigned via contrastive BLEURT/ROUGE
judge matching HARP's rubric.  \textbf{Training vs.\ inference:} the 10-beam
protocol is used exclusively to build the training population and capture
the full variance space for HOSVD.  At inference time, our pipeline requires
only a single forward pass to project and classify --- the 10-beam search
is purely for HARP-compatible evaluation, not for deployed detection.

\subsection{Hallucination Rate as a Predictor of Full-Beam Gain}

Full-beam always dilutes the hallucination rate (adds more correct than
hallucinated beams from known prompts).  Whether this helps or hurts
depends on the pre-dilution truthful beam count.

  & NQ-Open  & 99.0\% & 96.8\% & $-$2.2pp & \textcolor{upgreen}{$+$\textbf{20.2\%}} \\
  & TriviaQA & 91.5\% & 79.7\% & $-$11.8pp & \textcolor{downred}{$-$0.5\%} \\
  & TyDiQA   & 83.9\% & 51.4\% & $-$32.5pp & \textcolor{downred}{$-$0.3\%} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Hallucination rate dilution vs.\ AUROC gain.  Full-beam functions as
         a data-augmentation strategy: it disproportionately helps truthful-poor
         datasets (Qwen NQ-Open: only 353 truthful beams under early-stop, 1,138
         under full-beam; AUROC jumps 20.2\%).  Truthful-rich datasets
         (TyDiQA: already $>$3,700 truthful beams) gain little or regress.}
\end{table}

\noindent
\textbf{Key insight:} Full-beam acts as over-sampling for the minority class.
When the truthful beam count is very low ($<$1,000), adding 3--10$\times$ more
truthful examples significantly stabilises the HOSVD subspace.  When truthful
beams already number in the thousands, additional copies are redundant and the
diluted hallucination signal hurts the classifier.

\subsection{Grid Search over HOSVD Hyperparameters}

We swept tensor ranks $R_L \in \{5, 16, 32\}$, $R_D \in \{64, 256, 512\}$,
and eigenvector offset $\in \{0, 10, 64\}$ (dropping top-k highest-variance
components to test if reasoning signal lives in lower-variance modes).
Logistic Regression was used for fast iteration.

\noindent
\textbf{Finding:} The optimal configuration under Logistic Regression is
$R_L=5$, $R_D=64$, \textbf{offset=0}.  Expanding $R_D$ to 256 or 512
universally degraded LR AUROC.  Dropping the top-10 components (offset=10)
failed across all datasets.  \textbf{Disclaimer:} this optimum is bounded
by the linear assumptions of LR.  Larger hidden-dimension ranks may
introduce non-linear geometry that LR cannot exploit --- motivating our
shift to HARP-HOSVD fusion with non-linear classifiers.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The reasoning signal and semantic noise are \textbf{highly entangled}
        within the top 64 principal components of the hidden-state space.
  \item Pure HOSVD applied to raw hidden states has a \textbf{mathematical
        ceiling} ($\sim$0.79--0.83 AUROC, trailing HARP by 10--14 points).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
\textbf{Why:} Standard HOSVD maximises global variance.  The top eigenvectors
capture the LLM's structural, syntactic, and semantic structure---the
dominant signal.  The factual reasoning and hallucination signal lives in
the \textbf{low-variance null space} of the unembedding matrix
($\mathbf{W}_{\text{unemb}} \in \mathbb{R}^{V \times D}$), as demonstrated
by HARP.  Our next step is HARP-HOSVD fusion: pre-filtering hidden states
through the reasoning subspace (bottom 5\% of $\mathbf{W}_{\text{unemb}}$'s
right singular vectors) before HOSVD projection.

\noindent
\textbf{Key Observations:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{HOSVD is the second-best method on 5 of 7 completed pairs.}
        On Qwen, HOSVD ranks 2nd on TruthfulQA (78.2), TriviaQA (86.2), and
        TyDiQA (81.4).  On LLaMA, HOSVD ranks 2nd on NQ-Open (83.3) and
        TruthfulQA (79.6).  LLaMA TyDiQA-GP is pending.  HARP claims \#1 on
        all completed pairs.

  \item \textbf{On Qwen-2.5-7B, HOSVD outperforms all non-HARP baselines}
        on TruthfulQA (78.2 vs.\ 63--64), TriviaQA (86.2 vs.\ 76--85),
        and TyDiQA (81.4 vs.\ 30--75).  NQ-Open (73.5) trails EigenScore
        (78.9) by 5.4 points.

  \item \textbf{Cross-model consistency:} TruthfulQA AUROC is nearly identical
        across models (77.2 vs.\ 77.4), suggesting the HOSVD subspace captures
        a model-agnostic hallucination signature.

  \item \textbf{No multiple sampling required.} Unlike HARP and HaloScope
        (marked ``Single'' in HARP Table~1), HOSVD extracts features from
        a single forward pass; the 10-beam generation is only for the
        HARP-compatible rubric, not for feature extraction.

  \item \textbf{Compression efficiency.} HOSVD compresses raw $(L, T, D)$
        hidden states (up to $32 \times 10 \times 4096 \approx 1.3$M entries)
        into a 320-dimensional core vector ($\sim$4000$\times$ reduction)
        while retaining discriminative power.
\end{enumerate}
